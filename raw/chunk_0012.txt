### Cervical Medial Branch Block Procedure Detail:

A time-out was performed. After obtaining informed consent, the patient was placed in the prone position in the procedure room. The skin was prepped and draped in sterile fashion. Under fluoroscopy a 27 gauge x 1.5 inch spinal needle was inserted at the lateral portion of the cervical facet along the course of the median nerve. Position of the needle was placed fluoroscopic guidance with alternating AP, lateral and oblique views as warranted. At each level, 0.5 to 1 mL of local anesthetic was injected (no heme or CSF was aspirated prior to injection). The needle was removed. Sterile bandages were applied to the injection site and the patient was brought to the post-procedure waiting area. After being observed for a period of time, post-procedure care instructions were reviewed with the patient and a hand out was provided to them. The patient was also instructed to monitor the site for signs of infection, and then the patient was discharged in stable condition.
